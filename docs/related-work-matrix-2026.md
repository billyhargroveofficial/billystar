# Related-work matrix 2026: Causal Carrier Plane

Matrix ID: `RW-CCP-2026-07-16-v5`. Literature snapshot: 2026-07-16. Status:
bounded primary-source comparison, not a claim of priority or an exhaustive
systematic review.

Companion documents: [CCP architecture](architecture-causal-carrier-plane.md)
and the [claims ledger](claims-ledger.md). The matrix bounds `ARC-001`; the
architecture and ledger remain authoritative for implementation status and
evidence-tier decisions.

## Scope and comparison rules

The matrix compares the Causal Carrier Plane (CCP) with primary work that
directly constrains at least one of its threat, measurement, topology,
adaptation, reliability or control-plane decisions. It does **not** treat a new
combination of known mechanisms as automatically novel. Absence from this
curated snapshot is not evidence that prior art does not exist.

The two decision columns use these definitions:

- **Causal intervention — yes:** a deliberate path/protocol impairment or
  topology treatment with a counterfactual comparison is central to the work;
  **partial:** controlled perturbations or A/B evaluation exist, but not as an
  evidence gate for carrier promotion; **no:** the core result is observational,
  classificatory, generative or prescriptive.
- **Adaptive selection — yes:** runtime evidence selects among alternative
  carriers or topologies; **partial:** the system generates variants, refreshes
  endpoints or performs a fixed phase switch, but does not run evidence-gated
  multi-carrier selection; **no:** it neither selects carriers nor topologies.

“Overlap” means a mechanism or constraint CCP reuses or must test. “Non-overlap”
is a hard claim boundary: CCP cannot cite that work as proof of the listed
property.

evidence and do not establish project novelty.

## Bounded novelty hypothesis

`H-NOV-1` (`E0`, open): a system may make a bounded systems contribution if a
**single enforceable control loop** simultaneously:

1. represents heterogeneous carriers by explicit topology, termination,
   capability and failure-domain metadata;
2. selects or rejects a carrier at runtime under a signed, expiring,
   rollback-protected, per-traffic-class policy; and
3. permits promotion from shadow to canary only when a preregistered paired
   intervention and claims ledger support the scoped causal effect.

The hypothesis concerns this integration and its evaluation. It claims no
novelty for ML-KEM/X25519, AEAD framing, any individual carrier, traffic
classification, protocol generation, padding, migration, rendezvous, FEC,
congestion control, causal inference or adaptive routing in isolation. Version
5 additionally excludes novelty for FEP/OKey-exchange definitions, generic
traffic shaping, endpoint/key transparency, Happy-Eyeballs racing, QUIC/MPTCP
multipath and path-aware selection. The only remaining candidate delta is the
joint enforceable loop, and even that remains `E0` pending systematic search.

The current repository does not yet instantiate the full hypothesis: the
control-plane skeleton is offline/shadow, carrier implementations are not wired
to live TUN selection, replayed evidence is not bound to a trusted signed
manifest/producer/artifact chain, and no `E3` result exists. Run-ID deduplication
prevents duplicate admission only; it does not authenticate attribution. The
only acceptable wording at this stage is “we are evaluating an evidence-gated
carrier-selection architecture.”

Phase-4 sketches for a threshold service registry/transparency layer,
FEP/OKey-exchange analysis, a causal multipath portfolio and bounded shaping are
explicit adaptations/inferences, not implemented mechanisms. In particular,
the current `86400 s` key/pin overlap preserves rollout continuity but a
compromised `Active` signer can still add an attacker pin alongside the old pin.

## Falsifiers and closure conditions

`H-NOV-1` is falsified or narrowed if any of the following holds:

- `F-NOV-1`: prior work before this snapshot implements the same three-part
  loop at comparable granularity; the matrix must then name that work and move
  the claim to engineering replication or a narrower delta.
- `F-NOV-2`: the implemented policy gate does not mechanically control
  selection/rollback, so the ledger is merely documentation rather than part of
  the system.
- `F-NOV-3`: carrier choice is not informed by paired causal evidence, or the
  experiment cannot separate path effect from implementation competing risks.
- `F-NOV-3a`: scope, timestamps, carrier capabilities, failure domains or
  outcomes remain caller assertions rather than fields bound to a trusted
  signed manifest and producer/artifact provenance.
- `F-NOV-4`: the contribution reduces to a fixed QUIC migration, endpoint
  rotation, protocol variant or congestion controller already covered by one
  row above.
- `F-NOV-5`: a broader database/forward-backward citation search finds an
  equivalent architecture omitted from this bounded snapshot.

The hypothesis may advance beyond `E0` only after all of these closure
conditions are recorded:

- reproducible search protocol across at least IEEE Xplore, ACM Digital
  Library, USENIX, NDSS, PoPETs/FOCI, IETF/IRTF and citation graphs, with exact
  queries, dates and inclusion/exclusion decisions;
- independent reviewer checks both the matrix and rejected candidates;
- the runtime loop, signed policy semantics and fail-closed behavior are
  implemented and tested rather than inferred from design prose;
- a verifier authenticates manifest, producer identity, binary/artifact hash
  and evidence scope before a result can affect admission; run-ID uniqueness is
  treated only as replay/deduplication state;
- at least one preregistered `E3` carrier effect and an explicit negative result
  are linked from the claims ledger.

Even after closure, permitted language remains scoped: “evidence supports this
integration/evaluation contribution under the reviewed corpus.” The words
“first,” “unblockable,” “undetectable,” “universal” and “does not exist
elsewhere” remain prohibited without separately sufficient evidence.
